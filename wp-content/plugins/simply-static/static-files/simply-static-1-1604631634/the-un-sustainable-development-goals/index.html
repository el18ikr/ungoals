Sustainable development has become a focus of attention because of global population growth and concern over the impact of climate change. It is widely recognised that sustainable development requires the needs of the environment, society and the economy to intersect. Unit 4 made the case that the industrial revolution and coal power led to a dramatic increase in atmospheric CO2 and it is appropriate now to explore how the fourth industrial revolution could provide solutions for a sustainable future. Climate change is just one aspect of damage to the natural environment and engineering solutions are needed for almost every aspect of our sustainable future.

Professor Jon Lovett, Chair in Global Challenges in the School of Geography in University of Leeds introduces the importance of sustainability by explaining what is meant by Spaceship Earth and how it relates to sustainable economics.

Sustainable development requires international cooperation. The UN Sustainable Development Goals were formally published in 2015 in the strategy document “Transforming our World: The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development”.
