The linear, re-use and circular economy (linear.png)

The current model where products mostly end up in landfills is referred to as a linear economy. Even a re-use economy still results in a significant landfill. A circular economy is one in which the raw materials that enter the production chain are used over and over again, promoting sustainability and reducing environmental damage.

The Circular Economy 100 Network is an initiative from The Ellen MacArthur Foundation which boasts an impressive array of global companies amongst its members and partners. Ellen MacArthur launched the charity in 2010 after her successful yachting career, during which she held the world record for the fastest solo circumnavigation of the globe.

The foundation’s mission is to accelerate the transition to a circular economy by promoting three principles:

  • Address waste and pollution in the design phase
  • Reuse, repair and remanufacture to keep products and materials in use
  • Mimic natural systems, for example by creating solutions where waste products from a product or process are processed for use as fertiliser
